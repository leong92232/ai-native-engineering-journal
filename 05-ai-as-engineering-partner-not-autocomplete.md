# How I Use AI as an Engineering Partner, Not Autocomplete

I start using AI coding after my staff spent his own money to buy credit for vibe coding. That's what pull me in.

I'm a manager, I don't have to be hands on much, so AI coding is not essential to me. Company also not ready for it, no policy, no guidance.

First I use AI with purpose — ask it create a wireframe, get what I need, move on. Then I use it more and more.

I use it for auto coding, I check the quality, but the speed is too fast for normal review to catch up. I change my way. I look into how Andrej Karpathy work, he's the one who name "vibe coding," but he also serious about the discipline side of it.

I realise the key of using AI, in my way, is:

1. Need to know what I want to build. If not sure, or missing detail, brainstorm first, before I ask AI to build anything.

2. Always document. Architecture, design document, process flow. PRD is new to me, most PRD I see is too little detail. So I ask for more — I grill AI for detail, same way Karpathy grill the model, until the gap show up, then I close the gap on paper, before any code.

3. Let AI check AI's code, without memory, so it don't just agree with its own mistake. Avoid hallucination confirming itself.

None of this is really a new lesson about AI.

Same lesson I keep learning about system — if you don't decide where the boundary is, something else decide it for you.

I just didn't notice it apply to how I use AI too.
