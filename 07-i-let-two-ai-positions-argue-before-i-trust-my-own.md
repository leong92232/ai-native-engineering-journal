# I Let Two AI Positions Argue Before I Trust My Own

At some point, "ask AI to critique this" stop being enough for me.

Because critique from a neutral voice is still soft. It point out something, then it hedge, then it also say "but this could work too." Not useful when I need a real opinion, not a balanced one.

So I stop asking a neutral AI. I start assigning it a position.

Not "review this PRD." Instead: sit at a trading desk, or think like a quant, and give me your weakest point in this design. Not "review this architecture." Instead: look at this the way an engineer with a very high bar and low patience would look at it. Not "grade this essay nicely." Instead: grade this the way a strict exam marker would, point out every mistake.

Different position, different critique come out. Same document, three completely different set of problem get raised, depend on who I ask it to be.

That alone already useful. But then I go one more step.

I don't just take one position's answer and trust it. I take two position, and let them argue against each other, in front of me.

One side push a case. Other side push back, find the hole in first side's argument. Not to reach agreement — I don't need agreement. I want to see, when pushed, which side's point still hold, and which one collapse the moment someone push on it.

I sit as the judge. Not just receiving an answer — watching an argument, and deciding which point survive.

This take more time than one answer. But one answer, from one neutral voice, is easy to accept quietly and move on. An argument in front of me, I can't skip past it — I have to actually decide who's right, which mean I have to actually understand the problem, not just accept a conclusion.

I don't want AI to hand me a position.

I want to watch two position fight, so mine come out stronger from having survived one.
