# AI Made My Team Faster. It Also Made Bad Decisions Faster.

The first sign wasn't in any report. It was one of my staff quietly spending his own money on AI coding credits.

No policy told him to. Company wasn't ready, no guidance, nobody approved anything. He just found it useful enough to pay for it himself.

That's when I knew this already arrived, whether we're ready or not.

So I went in myself to see what it actually does. And the honest answer: yes, it's fast. Real fast. Things that took days come back in hours. Wireframe, working code, draft document — the output just keep coming.

But after a while I notice the part nobody put in the demo.

AI doesn't just make good work faster. It make every decision faster — including the wrong ones.

Before AI, a bad design decision was slow. Someone had to actually build it, and somewhere along those days of building, someone usually notice something feel off. The slowness itself was a kind of safety net. Bad idea die halfway because building it take long enough for doubt to catch up.

Now a bad idea become working code in one afternoon. It look finished. It look confident. It even come with nice comments. And review can't catch up — the speed of producing pass the speed of any human checking. So either you rubber-stamp what you didn't really read, or you become the bottleneck everyone wait for.

Neither one is okay. And here's the thing — the problem was never the speed.

Speed just remove the delay that used to hide our missing discipline. If a team never had clear boundaries — who decide what get built, what get written down before coding, who check whose work — AI doesn't create that problem. It expose it. Faster than anyone expect.

What I changed wasn't the tool. I changed where the slowness live.

Decide what we're building before asking AI to build it. Write the intent down first — not after. And never let the same context that produce the work also be the one that check it.

The slow part didn't disappear. It moved to the front, where it's cheap. A wrong decision caught on paper cost a meeting. The same wrong decision caught in production cost a lot more — and with AI, it get to production faster than ever.

AI didn't make my team's judgment better or worse.

It just made the result of that judgment arrive much, much sooner.
